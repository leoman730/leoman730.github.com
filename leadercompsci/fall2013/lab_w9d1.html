Lab: Different Block Types in BYOB

In this lab, you will use your new knowledge of block types to build a command block, a reporter block, and a predicate block.

Directions:

  1. Open up BYOB to create a new file.

  2. Create a block that draws a polygon with a variable number of sides, and a variable side length. Your block should look like this:

    Once you have finished your block, test it out on the following inputs to make sure it works correctly:

    • A triangle with sides of length 100
    • A square with sides of length 200
    • A 12-gon with sides of length 30

  3. Create a block that calculates the minimum of two numbers ("min of __ and __"). Once you have finished your block, test it out on the following inputs to make sure it works correctly:

    • min of 5 and 10
    • min of -5 and 0
    • min of 7 and 7

  4. Create a block that says whether a number is between two other numbers. Your block should look like this:

    Once you have finished your block, test it out on the following inputs to make sure it works correctly:

    • Is 10 between 5 and 20?
    • Is 10 between 20 and 5?
    • Is 10 between 0 and 5?
    • Is 10 between 5 and 10?
  5. Once you've completed your lab, save your BYOB file and email it as an attachment to help@leaderscompsci.com.
