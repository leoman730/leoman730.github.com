Hello World!

Set up a Scratch account to start writing and sharing code online! Follow the steps below. You will turn in your lab by submitting the form in the last step.

  1. Go to http://scratch.mit.edu/
  2. Click on the "Join Scratch" button at the top of the screen, and follow the steps. Choose a username and password that you will remember.
  3. Once you have created an account, click on "Create" in the top navigation bar to create a new program.
  4. Choose a sprite and backdrop for your program. You can draw a new sprite or choose one from the library.
  5. Using the "Say ___ for ___ seconds" block, make your sprite say "Hello world!".
  6. Next, using the "When this sprite clicked" and "Ask ___ and wait" blocks, make your sprite do the following whenever it's clicked: Ask the user for their name, and then say "Hello!".
  7. Use the rest of the lab time to explore the features of Scratch. One idea: Make your sprite say the user's name that they typed in.
  8. Share your program by hitting the share button. (You will need the URL to your shared remix for the lab submission form.)
  9. During the last 5 minutes of class, fill out this form to submit your lab: https://docs.google.com/forms/d/1ZGVYF6idmx-FTEpXIh1Sue31O1ajAXmjpFZmc9K3q5E/viewform
