Random Variables

With random variables, you can create games with unanticipated challenges. Let's build a game where Mario has to dodge enemies and collect rewards that are falling from the sky

  1. Go to http://scratch.mit.edu/ and log in to your account.
  2. Remix this project. Don't forget to share it!
  3. There are 4 variables that you will use in the game: marioSpeed, rewardSpeed, enemySpeed, and score.
  4. Add scripts to make Mario move back and forth when you press the left and right arrow keys. Instead of moving a constant number of steps, though, make Mario move marioSpeed steps each time.
  5. Next, add a script to make the reward continously fall from the top of the screen. It should fall at rewardSpeed, and when it touches the ground, it should disappear, wait for 1 second, and then fall again from the top of the screen.
  6. Next, make the enemy fall just like the reward. It should fall at enemySpeed.
  7. Next: Mario should gain 1 point every time he collects (touches) a reward, and lose 1 point every time he hits an enemy.
  8. Finally, add some randomness into your game! Instead of falling from the same place at the top of the stage each time, the enemy and reward should fall from a different spot each time.
  9. At the end of class, fill out this form to submit your lab.
