An UX PROJECT BY PATRICK HUANG
Through in-depth research, I found that shoppers prioritize deals and convenience. Leveraging this insight, I designed, prototyped and tested a shopping app to help malls see an increase in sales and customer satisfaction.

Survey

From a survey I conducted , I discovered that users put a lot of focus on deals and sales when they are shopping at the mall. They also trying to find way to help them to buy the right product.

From other research :
http://www.intomobile.com/2013/04/23/heres-smartphones-change-way-we-shop/
- 45% of user use smartphone for in-store product research and browsing
- 49% of users performed a price comparison with other retailers
- 1/4 smart phone user were promoted to go in store after seeing an advertisement

Competitive Analysis

From my competitive analysis, I learned that although users want to use some sort of mobile application during their mall visit, there doesn’t seem to be one that can meet their expectations.

Pros:

  • Voice Control
  • Turn by turn direction
  • Booking for movies and resturants

Cons:

  • Mall info out of date
  • Report function not working

Pros:

  • Social media integrations
  • Parking assistants (reminder, location pin)

Cons:

  • Huge power consumption
  • Unintuitive map navigation

Target Users

By synthesized the data I collected from survey and user interviews, I created two personas that the users in the application.

Busy Emily

“Finding deals and save time is very important when I shopping at the mall.”

Background:

35, female, Fashion designer College married with 2 kids

Goals:

- Get thing done fast
- Buying the right product

Motivation:

Emily is a busy person who has a full time job as a fashion designer. She always spend money smartly. Shopping at mall has been a monthly routine for her family. 

Frustrations:

- She has a difficult time to find good deals at the mall - Shopping at the mall is time consuming and confusing

Aggressive Greg

“Come and take advantage of the big deals at my store.”

Goals:

- Get more customers to his store
- Increase the reputation of his store

Background:

40, male, store owner College, married no kid

Frustrations:

- He couldn’t find a cheap and easy way to promote his store

Motivation:

Greg is a store owner at the mall selling children clothings. His store didn't make a good profit for the past couple months. He is very desperate to find way to advertise his store in order to boost store performance.

How users could use the application

Search for coupons

A user want to buy some new clothes and she uses EasyShopper to help her locate deals. (See scenario 1)

Create a promotion

A store owner wants to promote a sale and he wants to submit a coupon deal to EasyShopper so to attract more customers. (See scenario 2)

Get store Direction

A user want to use EasyShopper to get to one of her favorites store. (See scenario 3)

Paper prototyping and Usability Testing

Taking into account from what I learn from research, I created a set of paper prototype. To validate my idea and concept. I invited 4 users to participate in an usability test session.

From the test, I found out:

  • 2 out of 4 users found issue with navigation
  • 4 out of 4 users were expecting there an easier way to find deals
  • 4 out of 4 users found bookmarking icons confusing
  • 3 out of 4 users didn’t know how to use direction assistant
Taking valuable feedback from users in the first round of testing, lo-fi digital wireframes were created

Usability Testing Round 2

I invited 3 users to perform a more thorough usability test session on the lo-fi digital wireframes.

From the usability test, I found out:

  • 2 out of 3 users feel the global search is too broad
  • 2 of 3 users suggest to simplify favorite items listing
  • 2 of 3 users not sure what’s next once the favorite icon is clicked

Conclusion

From the research we know that while people at the mall, they wants realtime time shopping assistant, deals and conveniences. The usability tests reveal and verify that if an mobile application can help provide an easy, real time assistant for users during their shopping mall visit that focuses on deals, conveniences, and strategic shopping, then shoppers can be more happy and the mall will see an increase in foot-traffic, sales.

The next step will be taken the design to the next level and to start work on marketing campaign and the application development.
